Exploring Home Wiring Planning

Before we get into the main activities of the day, we’re going to take a few minutes to talk about the kinds of home furnishings we want in our Contrast Houses. Do we want a dishwasher? Clothes washer? Dryer? Lights? Ceiling fans? These questions will effect how we ultimately design the wiring plan for our homes.

To do this, you’re going to need the floorplans to the Berea Contrast House (PDF). You might want to print one or more copies of the first page so you can mark on it as you brainstorm about the layout of the house.

Collaborative Reading

After deciding on furnishings, we will do some collaborative reading. The purpose of reading and working through this particular text with a partner is to make sense of the process of designing a home wiring layout.

This process for structured, pairwise reading looks like the following:

  1. Skim the next paragraph.
  2. If it does not look critical, mark it “RETURN” and go back to step 1.
  3. If it does look critical, read the paragraph more closely.
  4. With your partner, identify the “big idea” in the paragraph.
  5. Together, identify any questions you have; write them down.
  6. Go back to step 1.

The document we will be reading is about developing a plan for wiring your own home. Along with your partner, you will be developing a plan for the wiring of two rooms in your Contrast House. This is the first step: developing an understanding of the issues involved, and the appropriate way in which to annotate your home wiring diagram.
